I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Biology at Duke University, working in the lab of Dr. Fay-Wei Li. My research focuses on the evolution of non-seed plants — the ferns, lycophytes, mosses, liverworts, and hornworts.

A central thread of my work is the evolution of organellar RNA editing, a post-transcriptional process in which specific pyrimidines in plastid and mitochondrial transcripts are exchanged, altering the encoded protein sequence. This phenomenon is widespread yet highly variable across land plant lineages, raising fundamental questions about why it evolved, how it is maintained, and whether it serves adaptive functions. I combine phylogenetic, comparative genomic, and experimental approaches to address these questions, and I am also interested in the biotechnological potential of plant RNA editing machinery as a platform for programmable base editing tools.

I completed my PhD at Duke University in December 2025 under the mentorship of Dr. Kathleen Pryer. I am broadly interested in applying phylogenetic tools to illuminate the evolutionary history of seed-free plants — a group that remains understudied relative to their remarkable diversity and ecological importance.
